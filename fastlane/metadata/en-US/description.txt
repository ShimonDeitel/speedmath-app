SpeedMath is math practice with nothing between you and the next question. No account, no setup screen — one big button starts a round.

Every question is generated on the spot, from first-grade counting up through university-level integrals, determinants, and limits, across 130 levels that track ten per grade. Answer in Type mode on a keypad built for math — digits, decimals, negatives, and fractions — or switch to Speed mode and just say the answer out loud; SpeedMath listens on-device and times how long it takes you. Get it right or wrong, you always see a clear worked solution afterward, and your level moves up or down based on how fast and how accurate you've been.

Your progress, streaks, and "performing at Grade X" estimate live entirely on your device — there's no account to create and nothing to sync.

The free version is supported by ads. SpeedMath Pro, an auto-renewing subscription at $4.99 per month, removes every ad and unlocks "Explain It" — a plain-language AI tutor that walks through any question a different way when the built-in steps aren't clicking.

No login. No streak-breaking penalties. Just questions, a clock, and how fast you can go.
